LATA MANGESHKAR

The voice of India

Lata Mangeshkar is an Indian playback singer, and occasional music-composer.

Born - 28 September 1929 Indore, India
Died - 6 February 2022 (aged 92) Mumbai, Maharashtra, India


Lata Mangeshkar was a legendary Indian playback singer known for her distinctive voice and a vocal range that extended over more than three octaves. . Her career spanned eight decades, and she recorded songs for the Hindi film industry of the 1940s and beyond.

Here are some key facts about her:
Lata Mangeshkar was born on September 28, 1929, in Indore, British India (present-day Madhya Pradesh, India). She was trained in music from age five by her father Pandit "Deenanath Mangeshkar", was a classical singer and theater actor, a disciple of the Gwalior gharana, and she was also tutored by maestros such as Aman Ali Khan Sahib and Amanat Khan. As a teenager, she struggled to help support her family and to establish herself as a playback singer in the Hindi film industry, at a time when the profession was dominated by such divas as Shamshad Begum and Noor Jehan. After Mangeshkar recorded the hit “Uthaye ja unke sitam” in Andaz (1949), her destiny was sealed. From that point on, she voiced the musical parts for every major leading lady, representing every generation of Bollywood actresses. She recorded over 30,000 songs in 14 Indian languages, making her the most recorded voice in history. Lata Mangeshkar was also a music composer and producer. She produced four films: Vaadal (Marathi), Jhaanjhar (Hindi), Kanchan Ganga (Hindi), and Lekin... (Hindi). She was awarded numerous honors throughout her career, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award, in 2001. Lata Mangeshkar passed away on February 6, 2022, in Mumbai, India, at the age of 92.


Awards and Recognitions

Lata Mangeshkar has won several awards and honours, including Bharat Ratna (India's Highest Civilian Award), Padma Bhushan (1969), Padma Vibhushan (1999), Dadasaheb Phalke Award (1989), Maharashtra Bhushan Award (1997), NTR National Award (1999), Bharat Ratna (2001), ANR National Award (2009), three National Film Awards, and 12 Bengal Film Journalists' Association Awards. She has also won four Filmfare Best Female Playback Awards. In 1969, she made the unusual gesture of giving up the Filmfare Best Female Playback Award, in order to promote fresh talent. She was later awarded Filmfare Lifetime Achievement Award in 1993. In 1984, the State Government of Madhya Pradesh instituted the Lata Mangeshkar Award in honour of Lata Mangeshkar. The State Government of Maharashtra also instituted a Lata Mangeshkar Award in 1992.
